LEARNING PHOTOSHOP

I am not completely new to Adobe Photoshop, but having learned it by myself, I haven’t really gotten all the smart shortcuts and smart things to do. After watching the 5 hour long Lynda course, I concluded that it was good to know all these things, but that there was no way I was going to remember all of them. It’s easier when you have it in your hands, and you have the time to play with it and figure stuff out yourself.

As I mentioned, I have done some stuff in Photoshop before; spot removal, cropping, changing background, combining photos, creating fake snow. All this I have learned through Youtube tutorials, so I had a good basic understanding of the program and its tools.

With this being said, it was nice to start from the bottom and move up from there. Walking through the basic tools on the class also helped understanding the last things, which I might have missed during the video course. And then just playing with it afterwards, refreshing what I already knew, was such fun. I also had great help in some of my classmates, who knew a bit more about the program than me. We had to do a little Photoshop project in class, and I chose the task to “merge two tv-shows and create a poster for it”. Of course I chose three, to challenge myself. The processes themselves weren’t that complicated, but I usually find my own way to do a task, even though it might not be the right or the easiest way. But broken down the image was made by: selection to layer, layer masks, making the top layer see-through in some areas, slight tone curve adjustments, and then adding the text in the right fonts.

I’m already excited to try new methods and doing more Photoshop projects.

/ Trine
